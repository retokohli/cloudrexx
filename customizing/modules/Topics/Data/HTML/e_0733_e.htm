Change of ownership

If there is a change of owner of the object of the contract, the rights and obligations arising from the insurance contract will be transferred to the new owner. The new owner can reject the transfer of the contract in a written declaration up to 30 days after the change of ownership. The insurance company can terminate the contract within 14 days after being notified of the new owner. The contract ends no later than 30 days after termination.

For motor vehicle liability insurance Art. 67 SVG applies as a special law governing the transfer of ownership. The motor vehicle liability insurance is transferred with all rights and obligations to the new owner. The new owner may end the contract if he/she proves that he/she has concluded an insurance contract with another company. Upon change of ownership, the premium remains due only until dissolution of the contract.
Purchase, procedures under company law, inheritance, exchange, gift inter vivos, auctioning, expropriation etc. are all legal reasons for change of ownership.
