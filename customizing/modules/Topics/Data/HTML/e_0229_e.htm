Departure gain

A departure gain results from the capital sum released (policy reserve) on the departure of an insured person from an occupational benefit plan. For a benefit plan having concluded collective insurance, the departure gain is equal to the difference between the surrender value retained by the insurance company and the occupational mobility credit. In occupational benefit plans only guaranteeing the BVG/LPP minimum there is no departure gain since each insured person leaving the plan withdraws his total vested retirement assets.
