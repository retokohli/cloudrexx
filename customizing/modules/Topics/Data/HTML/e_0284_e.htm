Surrender value

Only endowment insurances which consist of both risk and savings components and thus generate an actuarial reserve have a surrender value. The law refers to insurances for specific insured events (VVG/LCA 90.2). Part of each premium paid for an endowment insurance is invested by the company as a savings premium which accrues interest. Another part of the premium is required for risk coverage, and an additional part for ongoing costs. The one-time policy acquisition costs arising on conclusion of the contract are distributed over all subsequent premiums, and are thus only discharged at the end of the premium payment period. The General Conditions of Insurance contain the rules by which the surrender value is calculated on the actuarial reserve. In case of doubt, the policyholder has the right to request that the calculation be checked and verified free of charge by the Federal Office of Private Insurance.
