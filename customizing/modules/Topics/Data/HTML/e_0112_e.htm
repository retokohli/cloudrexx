Benefits in the context of succession

On the death of an insured person, insured benefits do not normally become part of the deceased's estate but are paid out directly to the beneficiaries. This situation may change subsequently where the heirs' shares in the estate are not respected. In such a case, the surrender value applicable at the time of death is included in the calculation of the heirs' shares. If the beneficiaries are descendants then the spouse, father and mother, grandparents or brothers and sisters have the right to the insured benefits even if they have repudiated succession - because it was burdened with debts, for instance.
