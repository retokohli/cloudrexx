Withdrawal

Withdrawal from the contract should not be confused with termination. A termination dissolves the contractual relationship in perpetuity, while a withdrawal effects the dissolution of the contractual relationship retroactively to a specific point in time. The consequence of withdrawal is that the insurer can request reimbursement of benefits which were indemnified subsequent to the existence of the reason for withdrawal. Examples include withdrawal as a result of a breach of the duty of disclosure or fraudulent substantiation of a claim.
