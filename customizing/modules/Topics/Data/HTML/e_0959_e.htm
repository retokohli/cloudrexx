Transport insurance

Transport insurance covers goods against risks incurred while being transported. It includes two main branches. The first, transport insurance/merchandise insurance covers the transported goods themselves. The second, forwarders liability insurance, covers the shipper and the freight carrier. In addition to these, a number of additional types of insurance fall under transport insurance: valuables insurance, transport on own account insurance, handling insurance and collision insurance for the modes of transportation. There are three variants of transport insurance: limited insurance, expanded insurance and all-risk insurance.
There are four different contract types in transport insurance: individual policies, general policies, flat-fee policies and sales turnover policies.
