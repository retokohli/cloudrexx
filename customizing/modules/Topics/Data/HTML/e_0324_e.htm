Survivorship life annuity certain

Should an insured person die before the end of the contractual period, the benefit is not paid out in the form of a capital sum (as with death risk insurance) but as an annual pension which continues to be paid up to the end of the agreed term. Hence the rightful claimant receives a replacement income at regular intervals up to a certain date. If he should also die before the term of the insurance then the right to the annuity passes to the person designated in the beneficiary clause or, where applicable, to his heirs. The life insurance company must, in any case, pay out annuities from the death of the insured person onwards. This is why their "value" can be calculated with accuracy and the beneficiary has the right - provided that the policyholder has made no provision otherwise - to receive payment as a capital sum of the discounted value of all future annuities
