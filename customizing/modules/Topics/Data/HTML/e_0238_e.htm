Mandatory accident insurance

The Federal Accident Insurance Law (UVG/LAA) came into force on 1 January 1984. This insurance is mandatory for all persons working in Switzerland except self-employed persons living in Switzerland and members of their families. The UVG/LAA covers professional and non-professional accidents and professional illness and provides cover for medical care, repayment of medical costs and payment of daily indemnities and pensions. A pensionable salary is considered to be the insured salary according to the Federal legislation on old-age and survival (AHV/AVS). Accident insurance (UVG/LAA) is administered either by the Swiss Accident Insurance Institute (SUVA) or by other recognized insurers. The premiums for the mandatory insurance are paid by the employer whereas those for non-professional accidents may be charged to the employee.
